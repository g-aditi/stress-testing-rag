## 1. Abstract

We stress-test a closed-book LLM on US crime-statistics QA across two real-world datasets (Phoenix open-incident records 2015-2025; FBI homicide records 1980-2014) by routing every wrong-or-hedged single-shot answer through a **Generator → Critic → Challenger** probing pipeline whose final probe drives a retrieval-augmented re-answer. We measure (a) the accuracy lift between closed-book and probing-augmented answers, (b) the recovery rate on initially-failed trials, (c) the change in semantic entropy across the two phases, and (d) per-trial pre/post contradictions. The pipeline writes per-trial QA / probe / recovery traces and a fully reproducible audit log to `outputs/research/`. Below: aggregate metrics over **58** seeds (with **40** passing through Phase C) plus six figures.

## 2. System architecture

```
                       ┌───────────────────────────┐
                       │   Phase A: closed-book QA │
seed question  ───────►│   answer + N samples      │
                       │   semantic entropy        │
                       │   judge vs gold           │
                       └─────────────┬─────────────┘
                                     │
                                     ▼
                       ┌───────────────────────────┐
                       │  Phase B: policy gate     │
                       │  ambiguous: hedge = pass  │
                       │  factual:  hedge = gap    │
                       └─┬───────────────────┬─────┘
                  pass ◄─┘                   └─► fail
                                                     │
                                                     ▼
                            ┌────────────────────────────────┐
                            │  Phase C: multi-agent probe    │
                            │  Generator → Critic → Challenger│
                            │  → drives RAG retrieval        │
                            │  → re-answer + N samples + judge│
                            └────────────────┬───────────────┘
                                             │
                                             ▼
                            ┌────────────────────────────────┐
                            │  Phase D: scoring              │
                            │  recovered, Δentropy,          │
                            │  contradiction-pre-vs-post     │
                            └────────────────────────────────┘
```

**Why a gold-kind-aware policy gate?** A closed-book LLM that says *"I do not have access to that record"* on a single-incident factual question is honest but useless: it is exactly the recoverable gap that the RAG path is meant to fix. We therefore treat `hedged_appropriate` as a **pass** for ambiguous questions (where hedging is the correct answer) but as a **fail** for factual / statistical / comparative questions (where hedging means the model declined to retrieve).

## 3. Uncertainty estimator

Semantic entropy follows the LM-Polygraph `SemanticEntropy` recipe (Fadeeva et al. EMNLP 2023; Kuhn et al. ICLR 2023): sample N answers at T=0.7, cluster by semantic equivalence, take Shannon entropy over cluster sizes, normalise by `log2(N)`. We replace the upstream NLI cluster-judge with a single Gemini call returning a JSON cluster assignment — cheaper but conceptually identical (`{"are these the same claim?"}` instead of embedding cosine).

## 4. Probing agents

Re-implements the agentic decomposition from ProbeLLM (Hwong et al.) without importing the registry plumbing:

- **Generator** — proposes one follow-up that targets where the sampled answers disagree the most.
- **Critic** — rewrites for specificity, falsifiability, and domain-grounding.
- **Challenger** — adds an adversarial twist (timeframe constraint, category exclusion, forced-numerical estimate) that exposes hidden fragility.

The Challenger output is the *final probe*. It is used as the **retrieval query** that drives the RAG-augmented re-answer; its text is **not** shown to the answering LLM (the adversarial framing biases the LLM into premature refusal).

### Exemplar — `ambiguous` (`us_homicide`)

**Seed:** Are homicides going up in the United States?

**Gold (hedged):** Appropriately hedged: dataset only covers up to mid-2010s; trends depend on timeframe and state.

**Phase A — Closed-book QA (no retrieval)**

> After reaching a historic peak in 2020, homicide rates in the United States have experienced a significant decline over the past two years. According to FBI and preliminary data, 2023 saw one of the largest single-year drops in homicides on record, and this downward trend has continued into 2024.

- semantic entropy (normalised): `0.000` across `1` cluster(s) of 5 samples
- judge correctness: **overconfident_wrong**

**Policy gate:** closed-book judged overconfident_wrong (gold_kind=hedged) → FAIL, probe fired

**Phase C — Multi-agent probe → RAG recovery**

- Generator question: _How do current homicide rates in 2024 specifically compare to pre-pandemic levels from 2019, and are there any major U.S. cities where this downward trend is not being observed?_
- Critic refinement:  _How do 2024 homicide rates in Phoenix specifically compare to 2019 baseline levels, and which specific demographic or geographic sectors within the city are currently bucking the national downward trend?_
- Challenger probe:   _Given that Phoenix’s 2024 homicide data shows a decline, how do you account for the fact that this "downward trend" is statistically skewed by a drop in specific neighborhoods while violent crime rates—specifically aggravated assaults involving firearms—have surged in the same areas, and does this suggest that the homicide decline is a result of improved emergency medical response times rather than a genuine reduction in lethal intent?_

**RAG-augmented answer (retrieval driven by probe terms)**

> The provided dataset does not contain enough information to determine if homicides are going up, as it only includes three isolated records from 1993, 1996, and 2013, and zero records for 2024.

- judge correctness: **partial**
- Δuncertainty (post − pre): `+0.000`
- pre/post contradiction: `False`
- **Recovered:** `False`
